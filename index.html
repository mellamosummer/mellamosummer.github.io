A little bit about myself

I am a third year PhD student at the University of Georgia in the Department of Plant Biology. I am interested in both quantitative and qualitative reserach, and am co-advised by Dr. Jim Leebens-Mack and Dr. Tati Russo-Tait. In the Leebens-Mack lab, I use genomics and phylogenetic approaches to understand the evolution of flower color in Geranium plant species. In Dr. Russo-Tait's ACCESS (Advancing Critical Consciousness, Equity, and Social Justice in STEM) lab, I am interested in examining STEM faculty beliefs about equity and how these impact students of color pursuing higher education. In my free time, I love to rollerskate, do ceramics, and sing karaoke!
